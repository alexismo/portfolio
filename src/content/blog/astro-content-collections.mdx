## Why content collections

Content collections give the blog a typed layer between raw MDX files and the pages that render them.
Each post's frontmatter is validated at build time, so broken metadata shows up early instead of in production.

### Schema as a contract

The schema defines what every post must include: title, description, publication date, category, tags, and author.
Optional fields like `updatedDate` and `ogImage` stay available without forcing every draft to fill them in.

### Draft posts stay out of the listing

Setting `draft: true` keeps a post out of public listings while still letting me preview it locally.
That makes it easier to iterate on layout and typography before publishing.

## A minimal frontmatter example

Every post starts with YAML frontmatter separated from the body by `---` delimiters.

```yaml
---
title: "My post title"
description: "A short summary for listings and SEO"
pubDate: "2026-07-03"
category: "Development"
tags: ["astro"]
draft: true
---
```
